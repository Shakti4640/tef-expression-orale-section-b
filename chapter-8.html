What a Strong Close Accomplishes

A strong close does four things:

1. It SUMMARIZES â€” It reminds the friend of the key benefits in a compact, compelling package. After 3-4 minutes of back-and-forth, a summary brings everything together.

2. It ASSUMES SUCCESS â€” It treats the "yes" as a done deal. Instead of asking "So... do you want to go?" (which invites "no"), it says "Great, I'm booking us in!" (which requires active resistance to refuse).

3. It CREATES ACTION â€” It moves from discussion to decision. "I'll reserve our spots tonight." "I'll pick you up at 10." The conversation shifts from abstract (should we go?) to concrete (here is what happens next).

4. It LEAVES A STRONG LAST IMPRESSION â€” The examiner's final memory of you is confident, energetic, and decisive. This colors their evaluation of the entire conversation.
