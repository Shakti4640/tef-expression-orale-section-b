Bonus Chapter: Family Obligations — "Faut que je m'occupe des enfants" and "Ma femme / Mon mari ne sera pas d'accord"

📦 SECTION SUMMARY

What this chapter covers: The complete toolkit for handling family-based objections — when your friend uses their spouse, children, parents, or family obligations as the reason they cannot join you. This category was not in the original TEF preparation materials, but it is one of the MOST COMMON objections in real conversation and appears regularly in exam scenarios. If the examiner plays a married friend, a parent, or someone with family responsibilities, these objections are almost guaranteed to surface.

What you will learn: 25 new pre-manufactured sentences (BONUS #B1–B25) covering five sub-blocks, 14 family-specific idioms, the critical distinction between REAL family obligations (which deserve respect) and family-as-shield excuses (which can be gently challenged), the art of INCLUDING the family in your argument rather than competing against them, and the technique of making your activity SERVE the family rather than take away from it.

Why this matters for your score: Family objections test something no other category tests: your ability to navigate RELATIONSHIPS BEYOND THE TWO OF YOU. When your friend says "My wife wouldn't like that," there is a third person in the conversation — invisible but powerful. How you handle that invisible presence reveals social intelligence, cultural awareness, and communicative sophistication. Candidates who say "Forget about your wife, come with me!" demonstrate B1 thinking. Candidates who say "Bring her along!" or "She'd want you to have fun too" demonstrate B2/C1 thinking.

Builds on: Chapter 9-10 (Time Objections — family obligations are often time-based at their core), Chapter 15 (Fatigue — parents are often exhausted), Chapter 19 (Fear — some family objections mask fear of partner disapproval), Chapter 21 (Commitment Fear — "What if my family needs me while I'm gone?")

Difficulty Level: ⭐⭐⭐⭐ (High — because you must show respect for family while still persuading, and cultural sensitivity matters enormously)

1️⃣ WHY FAMILY OBJECTIONS ARE A CATEGORY OF THEIR OWN

Every other objection category involves a negotiation between TWO people — you and your friend. Family objections introduce a THIRD PARTY into the conversation. Your friend is not saying "I don't want to." They are saying "Someone ELSE doesn't want me to" or "Someone ELSE needs me." This changes the dynamic completely.

3️⃣ BLOCK 1: CHILDCARE — "FAUT QUE JE M'OCCUPE DES ENFANTS"

What the Examiner Might Say

  • "Faut que je m'occupe des enfants." — Direct, practical, responsibility-based.
  • "J'ai personne pour garder les petits." — Logistics problem — no childcare available.
  • "Je peux pas laisser mes enfants tout seuls." — Parental duty and concern.
  • "Les enfants ont besoin de moi ce week-end." — Emotional obligation.
  • "Qui va s'occuper des gamins si je sors ?" — Practical question demanding a practical answer.
  • "Mon fils / Ma fille a un truc à l'école, je dois être là." — Specific child-related event.

Your Thinking Process

I hear: "Faut que je m'occupe des enfants"
    ↓
I recognize: FAMILY OBLIGATION — childcare logistics
    ↓
I do NOT: Say "Laisse-les, ils vont survivre !"
          (Irresponsible and dismissive of parenting)
    ↓
I DO:
    Step 1 — RESPECT: "C'est tout à fait normal, 
             t'es un super papa / une super maman."
    Step 2 — EXPLORE solutions:
             → Can someone else watch them? Partner, 
               grandparents, trusted friend, babysitter?
             → Can the kids COME with you?
             → Can the timing be adjusted around their schedule?
    Step 3 — REFRAME: "Prendre du temps pour toi fait 
             de toi un meilleur parent."
    Step 4 — ENGAGE: "On trouve une solution ensemble ?"

Complete Responses

#French SentenceStrategyThe Psychology Behind It
B1"Et si on emmenait les enfants ? Y a sûrement des trucs pour eux sur place, et comme ça tu profites aussi !"INCLUDE THE KIDSThis is often the simplest and most effective solution. Many activities are family-compatible — outdoor events, markets, festivals, parks, certain exhibitions. By suggesting the kids come, you eliminate the childcare problem entirely AND show that you care about the children too. The friend gets to enjoy the activity WITHOUT guilt.
B2"Ton mari / Ta femme peut pas les garder pendant quelques heures ? C'est juste un après-midi, pas une semaine !"PARTNER SHARINGThis suggests the other parent takes over temporarily. The phrase "c'est juste un après-midi" minimizes the ask — you are not requesting a week of solo parenting, just a few hours. This works when the friend's partner is available but the friend has not thought to ask.
B3"Et tes parents ? Ils seraient peut-être ravis de passer du temps avec leurs petits-enfants ! Ça fait plaisir à tout le monde."GRANDPARENT SOLUTIONBrilliant because it reframes the childcare arrangement as a GIFT to the grandparents, not a burden. Many grandparents WANT more time with grandchildren. You are not dumping the kids — you are creating a bonding opportunity for three generations simultaneously.
B4"Écoute, les enfants seront contents avec [partenaire / grands-parents / nounou] pendant trois heures. Et toi tu mérites une pause. Un parent reposé, c'est un meilleur parent."THE RECHARGE ARGUMENTThis is the most psychologically powerful argument for parents. Parental guilt is enormous — the feeling that every moment away from your children is a moment of failure. By explicitly stating "a rested parent is a better parent," you give them PERMISSION to take care of themselves. This is not selfishness — it is strategic self-care that SERVES the family.
B5"L'activité finit à 14h. T'as tout le reste de la journée avec tes enfants après. C'est juste la matinée !"TIME-BOXINGYou are showing that the activity does not consume the entire day. It fits INTO the family schedule rather than replacing it. Morning activity, afternoon with kids. Everyone gets what they need. The specific time ("14h") makes it concrete and manageable.
B6"Tu sais quoi, je connais une super baby-sitter. Je te file son numéro et c'est réglé !"PRACTICAL PROBLEM-SOLVINGSometimes the friend genuinely has no childcare solution and needs someone to provide one. By offering a concrete name and number, you remove the logistics barrier entirely. You are not just persuading — you are HELPING.
B7"C'est un événement familial en fait ! Y a plein de familles avec enfants. Les tiens vont se faire des copains pendant qu'on profite."FAMILY-FRIENDLY REFRAMEFor events that genuinely welcome families, this is perfect. You are transforming the objection into an advantage — the children will have fun too, make friends, run around. It becomes a family outing disguised as your friend's activity.

A Complete Exchange — Childcare Objection

Scenario: You want your friend to join a Saturday morning cooking workshop.

Examiner: "Un atelier cuisine samedi matin ? Impossible, j'ai les enfants. Mon mari travaille le samedi."

You (Sentence B3 + B4): "Ah mince. Et tes parents ? Ils habitent pas loin, non ? Je suis sûr(e) qu'ils seraient ravis d'avoir les petits pour la matinée. Ça leur fait plaisir et ça te libère quelques heures. Et franchement, tu mérites cette pause. T'es une maman formidable, mais même les super-mamans ont besoin de souffler. Un parent reposé, c'est un meilleur parent, tu le sais."

Examiner: "Mes parents sont occupés ce samedi..."

You (Sentence B5 + B6): "OK, et une baby-sitter ? L'atelier finit à midi — c'est trois heures, c'est rien. Je connais la fille de ma voisine, elle est super avec les enfants, les gamins l'adorent. Je te donne son numéro ce soir. Et puis imagine : tu rentres à midi avec un plat italien fait maison, les enfants sont contents, tout le monde mange bien. C'est gagnant-gagnant, non ?"

4️⃣ BLOCK 2: SPOUSE DISAPPROVAL — "MA FEMME / MON MARI NE SERA PAS D'ACCORD"

This is the most DELICATE sub-type. Your friend is invoking their partner's authority or opinion. You must navigate this without criticizing the partner, without undermining the relationship, and without making your friend feel controlled.

What the Examiner Might Say

  • "Ma femme ne sera pas d'accord." — Direct spouse veto.
  • "Mon mari va râler si je sors encore." — Implies tension about going out.
  • "On s'était dit qu'on passerait le week-end ensemble." — Couple plans exist.
  • "Ma copine / Mon copain veut qu'on reste tranquilles ce week-end." — Partner's preference for staying home.
  • "Si je sors samedi, ma femme va me faire la tête." — Fear of partner's negative reaction.
  • "Faut que je demande à mon mari / ma femme d'abord." — Needing permission (or using it as delay tactic).

The Two Possible Realities

When the examiner says "My partner won't agree," there are TWO possible realities, and you must navigate both:

RealityWhat Is Actually HappeningYour Approach
Genuine couple commitmentThey really DO have plans with their partner, or their partner really does need them at homeRESPECT the commitment. Suggest a different time. Or suggest INCLUDING the partner.
Partner-as-shieldThe friend is using their partner as an EXCUSE because they do not want to say "I don't want to go" directlyGENTLY challenge by reframing what the partner would actually think. "Je suis sûr(e) qu'il/elle comprendrait."

⚠️ YOU CANNOT KNOW WHICH REALITY IS TRUE. In the exam, always start by assuming the commitment is genuine. This shows respect. If the examiner keeps pushing the spouse objection, you can gradually shift to gentle challenges.

Complete Responses

#French SentenceStrategyThe Psychology Behind It
B8"Et si tu lui en parlais ? Je suis sûr(e) qu'il/elle comprendrait que t'as besoin de ce moment pour toi."ENCOURAGE COMMUNICATIONYou are not telling them to defy their partner. You are suggesting they TALK about it. The assumption "il/elle comprendrait" is generous — it frames the partner as reasonable and supportive. You are giving the partner credit rather than painting them as controlling.
B9"Invite-le / Invite-la ! Plus on est de fous, plus on rit. Comme ça, tout le monde en profite."INCLUDE THE SPOUSEThe most elegant solution. If the partner comes, there is no conflict. This works for social activities, cultural events, dining experiences, and many workshops. It also shows you WANT to know their partner, which is flattering.
B10"C'est que quelques heures ! Tu rentres à temps pour passer la soirée ensemble. Le meilleur des deux mondes !"TIME-BOXINGSame technique as with childcare — show that the activity fits AROUND the couple time, not instead of it. Morning with you, evening with partner. Everyone wins.
B11"Ta femme / Ton mari voudrait que tu sois heureux/heureuse aussi. Prendre du temps pour toi, c'est pas trahir ton couple."PERMISSION TO SELF-CAREThis addresses the GUILT directly. Many people in relationships feel that any time spent without their partner is somehow disloyal. By stating that a loving partner WANTS their happiness, you normalize individual activities within a relationship. Healthy couples have separate interests AND shared ones.
B12"Dis-lui que c'est moi qui insiste ! Comme ça c'est ma faute, pas la tienne. Je prends la responsabilité !"HUMOROUS BLAME-TAKINGLight, funny, and effective. You are offering to be the "bad influence" so your friend does not have to feel guilty. This works because it is clearly a joke — but underneath the humor, it genuinely offers to absorb the social cost. Many people use this in real life: "Tell your wife I kidnapped you!"
B13"Tu pourrais lui ramener quelque chose de l'activité ! Un petit cadeau, un souvenir. Elle/Il va adorer."THE GIFT STRATEGYTurning the outing into an opportunity to do something NICE for the partner. If it is a food market, bring back cheese. If it is a craft workshop, make something for the partner. If it is a trip, bring back a souvenir. The activity becomes an act of love, not an act of escape.
B14"Et si elle/il faisait un truc de son côté aussi ? Chacun sa sortie, et le soir vous vous racontez vos journées !"RECIPROCAL FREEDOMThis is sophisticated. Instead of one person going out while the other stays home (which creates resentment), BOTH partners get their own activity. Saturday morning: you go to the workshop, partner goes to yoga / shopping / brunch with their friends. Saturday evening: you reconnect and share stories. This is how healthy relationships actually work.

A Complete Exchange — Spouse Disapproval

Scenario: You want your friend to join a Saturday afternoon wine tasting.

Examiner: "Samedi après-midi ? Ma femme va pas être contente si je sors encore. La semaine dernière j'étais déjà sorti avec les collègues..."

You (Sentence B8 + B11): "Je comprends, et c'est bien que tu penses à elle. Mais est-ce que t'en as parlé ? Parce que je suis sûr que ta femme voudrait que tu profites aussi. Prendre un après-midi pour toi de temps en temps, c'est pas trahir ton couple. Au contraire, tu reviens de meilleure humeur, plus détendu, et c'est elle qui en profite aussi."

Examiner: "Quand même, on s'était dit qu'on passerait le week-end ensemble..."

You (Sentence B14 + B10): "Et si elle faisait un truc de son côté aussi pendant ce temps ? Genre elle va bruncher avec ses copines ou elle se fait une séance shopping tranquille. Comme ça, chacun son moment, et le soir vous vous retrouvez pour une super soirée ensemble. La dégustation finit à 17h — t'es à la maison pour 18h. Et en plus, je te connais, tu vas ramener une bouteille pour elle. Elle va t'adorer !"

Examiner: "Hmm, c'est vrai que vu comme ça..."

You (Sentence B12 + Close): "Écoute, dis-lui que c'est moi qui t'ai forcé ! Je prends toute la responsabilité. Et si elle veut, la prochaine dégustation on y va tous les trois. Mais samedi, c'est notre moment à nous. Allez, je nous inscris ?"

5️⃣ BLOCK 3: FAMILY EVENTS AND OBLIGATIONS — "ON A UN TRUC EN FAMILLE"

What the Examiner Might Say

  • "On a un repas de famille ce jour-là." — Pre-existing family commitment.
  • "C'est l'anniversaire de ma belle-mère." — Specific family event.
  • "Mes parents viennent nous rendre visite ce week-end." — Family visit.
  • "On doit aller chez mes beaux-parents." — In-law obligations.
  • "Ma sœur / Mon frère a besoin de moi ce jour-là." — Sibling responsibility.

Complete Responses

#French SentenceStrategyThe Psychology Behind It
B15"C'est à quelle heure ton truc en famille ? Peut-être qu'on peut faire l'activité avant ou après !"SCHEDULE AROUND ITFamily events rarely consume an entire day. A lunch at the in-laws is typically noon to 3pm. An activity in the morning (9-11am) or evening (6-8pm) could fit perfectly. By asking for the specific time, you look for a window rather than accepting a total block.
B16"C'est reportable ton repas de famille ? Parce que cette opportunité-là, elle attend pas."PRIORITY COMPARISONGently suggesting that a regular family dinner can be moved to another week, while this specific event is time-limited. Use this CAREFULLY — only for routine family meals, NEVER for birthdays, anniversaries, or significant events.
B17"Et si tu passais juste une heure ou deux avec nous, et après tu files chez ta famille ? Même un petit moment, c'est mieux que rien !"PARTIAL ATTENDANCEYou are not asking them to skip the family event. You are asking for a PORTION of their day. Come for an hour, then go to the family thing. This shows flexibility and respect for their commitment.
B18"OK, ce week-end c'est famille. Mais le week-end d'après, tu bloques la date pour moi ! Promis ?"GRACEFUL RESCHEDULESometimes the family obligation is genuinely immovable. When that is the case, the smart move is to ACCEPT it gracefully and immediately LOCK IN the next available date. This shows maturity and respect while keeping the plan alive.

6️⃣ BLOCK 4: PARENTAL GUILT — "JE PASSE PAS ASSEZ DE TEMPS AVEC MA FAMILLE"

This is the EMOTIONAL version of the family objection. The friend is not citing a specific logistics problem — they are expressing GUILT about time away from their family in general.

What the Examiner Might Say

  • "Je passe déjà pas assez de temps avec mes enfants." — Parental guilt.
  • "J'ai l'impression de négliger ma famille en ce moment." — General guilt about priorities.
  • "Les enfants grandissent tellement vite, je veux pas rater ça." — Time-scarcity anxiety.
  • "Ma femme / Mon mari dit que je suis jamais à la maison." — Partner's complaint being internalized.

Complete Responses

#French SentenceStrategyThe Psychology Behind It
B19"Je comprends totalement, et ça montre que t'es un parent formidable. Mais un parent qui prend soin de lui-même prend aussi soin de sa famille."VALIDATE + SELF-CARE REFRAMEThis is the most important sentence in this block. You START by affirming their devotion as a parent — "ça montre que t'es un parent formidable." You are not dismissing the guilt, you are honoring it. THEN you introduce the reframe: self-care IS family care. A burnt-out, frustrated, exhausted parent serves no one. A refreshed, happy, energized parent brings that energy HOME.
B20"Tes enfants ont besoin d'un papa / d'une maman épanoui(e). Si tu fais que travailler et t'occuper des autres sans jamais souffler, tu vas t'épuiser."THE BURNOUT WARNINGThis is a gentle reality check. Parents who never take time for themselves eventually burn out — and burnout hurts the WHOLE family. By pointing this out, you are not being selfish. You are being protective of the family's long-term well-being.
B21"Trois heures de pause, c'est pas négliger ta famille. C'est investir dans ton bien-être pour être encore meilleur(e) quand tu rentres."INVESTMENT LANGUAGEReframing the time away as an INVESTMENT rather than a loss. You are not spending three hours away from your family. You are investing three hours in becoming a better version of yourself for your family. The language of investment is powerful because it implies RETURNS.
B22"Tu sais ce que tes enfants retiendront ? Pas que tu étais là chaque seconde. Mais que tu étais heureux/heureuse et présent(e) quand t'étais là."QUALITY OVER QUANTITYThis addresses the deepest fear of guilty parents: "If I'm not there every moment, my children will suffer." Research consistently shows that children benefit more from a PRESENT, HAPPY parent for fewer hours than from a STRESSED, RESENTFUL parent who is physically there but emotionally absent. Quality of attention beats quantity of time.

7️⃣ BLOCK 5: NEEDING PARTNER'S PERMISSION — "FAUT QUE JE DEMANDE D'ABORD"

What the Examiner Might Say

  • "Faut que je demande à ma femme / mon mari." — Seeking approval.
  • "Je peux pas décider comme ça, faut qu'on en parle." — Couple decision-making.
  • "Je te dis ça quand j'aurai parlé à mon/ma conjoint(e)." — Delay tactic.

Complete Responses

#French SentenceStrategyThe Psychology Behind It
B23"Bien sûr, parles-en ce soir ! Mais les places sont limitées, donc essaie de me confirmer demain, d'accord ?"RESPECT + URGENCYYou respect the couple decision process AND create a deadline. You are not saying "decide now without your partner." You are saying "discuss it tonight, let me know tomorrow." This is mature and respectful while still maintaining momentum.
B24"Appelle-le / Appelle-la maintenant ! Je suis sûr(e) que ça va être un oui. Et si c'est non, au moins on aura essayé."IMMEDIATE ACTIONFor a more playful approach — suggest calling the partner RIGHT NOW. This works when the friend is using "I need to ask" as a delay tactic. If they actually call, you might get an immediate yes. If they refuse to call, it reveals that the objection might not be genuine.
B25"Écoute, tu me connais. Dis-lui que c'est avec moi, que c'est safe, que tu rentres à telle heure. Je suis sûr(e) qu'il/elle va dire oui."PERSONAL GUARANTEE TO THE ABSENT PARTNERYou are offering YOUR reputation as reassurance to the partner. "It's with me" — a trusted friend, not strangers. "You'll be back by X time" — specific and concrete. This gives your friend AMMUNITION for the conversation with their partner. You are helping them make their case.

9️⃣ COMPLETE SCENARIO — FAMILY OBJECTIONS IN A FULL CONVERSATION

Scenario: You want your friend to join a Saturday morning photography walk in the old town.

You (Opening): "Hé ! Tu te souviens quand tu disais que tu voulais apprendre la photo ? J'ai trouvé un truc parfait — une balade photo dans le vieux quartier samedi matin. Un pro nous guide, nous apprend les techniques, et on repart avec des photos magnifiques. Ça te dit ?"

Examiner (Childcare): "Samedi matin ? Impossible. Julie travaille le samedi, c'est moi qui ai les enfants."

You (Sentence B3 + B5): "Ah oui, c'est vrai. Et tes parents ? Ils adorent avoir les petits, non ? Ça leur ferait super plaisir ! Et la balade c'est de 9h à midi — tu récupères les enfants à 12h30, ça te laisse tout l'après-midi avec eux. Ils auront même pas le temps de s'apercevoir que t'étais parti !"

Examiner (Parental Guilt): "Je sais pas... je passe déjà pas beaucoup de temps avec eux en semaine avec le boulot..."

You (Sentence B19 + Idiom F11): "Je comprends, et ça montre que t'es un papa formidable, vraiment. Mais arrête de porter le monde sur tes épaules. Trois heures pour toi le samedi matin, c'est pas négliger tes enfants. Au contraire — tu reviens avec la pêche, tu leur montres tes photos, tu leur racontes ce que t'as appris. Ça leur donne un papa épanoui et passionné. C'est ça le meilleur exemple que tu puisses leur donner."

Examiner (Spouse concern): "Quand même, Julie risque de trouver que je pense qu'à moi..."

You (Sentence B11 + B14 + Idiom F5): "Julie t'aime et elle veut que tu sois heureux. Je suis sûr qu'elle comprendrait. Et puis, chacun son tour — la semaine prochaine c'est elle qui sort avec ses copines pendant que tu gères les enfants. C'est l'équilibre ! Propose-lui ça ce soir : 'Samedi je fais la balade photo avec [ton prénom], et le samedi d'après t'as ta matinée libre.' Je parie qu'elle va trouver ça super juste."

Examiner (Softening): "C'est pas bête comme arrangement..."

You (Close + Idiom F8): "Exactement ! Tu sais ce qu'on dit — qui veut voyager loin ménage sa monture. Si tu veux être un super papa sur le long terme, faut que tu prennes soin de toi maintenant. Allez, parles-en à Julie ce soir et confirme-moi demain. Je nous réserve deux places. Tu vas adorer, et Julie va adorer tes photos !"

1️⃣1️⃣ WHAT TO NEVER DO — FAMILY OBJECTIONS

❌ NEVER Say ThisWhy It Destroys the Conversation
"Oublie ta femme / ton mari pour une fois !"Positions you AGAINST their partner. The friend will always choose their partner over you. Always.
"Tes enfants vont survivre sans toi."Minimizes parental love. Even if true, it sounds cold and uncaring about children.
"T'es sous la coupe de ta femme / ton mari."Calls them controlled or weak. Insults their relationship. Conversation over.
"Tes parents peuvent bien garder les enfants, c'est leur rôle."Imposes obligations on people who are not in the conversation. Presumptuous and entitled.
"T'as pas besoin de sa permission pour sortir."Undermines the couple's decision-making process. Even if the friend agrees internally, they cannot say so without betraying their partner.
"Toujours ta famille, toujours des excuses."Accusatory. Frames their family as a problem rather than a priority. Destroys trust.
"Tes enfants s'en fichent, ils remarqueront même pas."Dismisses the parent-child bond. Even if children are resilient, this sounds callous.

💡 THE UNDERLYING PRINCIPLE: Every sentence you say about their family should pass this test: "If their partner or children could hear me right now, would they feel RESPECTED?" If the answer is no, do not say it.

1️⃣3️⃣ KEY TAKEAWAYS FROM THIS BONUS CHAPTER

1. Family objections introduce a THIRD PARTY into the conversation. Never position yourself against the family. Always include them, complement them, or serve them.

2. There are five sub-types: childcare logistics, spouse disapproval, family events, parental guilt, and needing partner's permission. Each requires a distinct approach.

3. For childcare: find alternative care (grandparents, babysitter, partner), include the kids, or time-box the activity around the kids' schedule.

4. For spouse concerns: encourage communication (B8), include the spouse (B9), offer reciprocal freedom (B14), or use humor to absorb blame (B12).

5. For parental guilt: validate their devotion FIRST, then reframe self-care as family care. "Un parent reposé est un meilleur parent" (B4) is the most powerful argument.

6. The QUALITY OVER QUANTITY argument (B22) addresses the deepest parental fear. Children remember a happy, present parent — not a parent who was physically there but emotionally exhausted.

7. Key idioms: on peut pas être au four et au moulin (can't be everywhere), lâcher du lest (ease up), porter le monde sur ses épaules (carrying too much), qui veut voyager loin ménage sa monture (take care of yourself to go the distance).

8. When family obligations are genuinely immovable (birthday, important event), GRACEFULLY RESCHEDULE (B18) and immediately lock in the next date. This shows maturity.

9. NEVER criticize the partner, minimize the children's needs, or accuse the friend of making excuses. Every statement about their family should pass the test: "Would their family feel respected hearing this?"

10. The ultimate strategy: make the activity SERVE the family. "Tu rentres avec un plat fait maison" / "Tu leur montres tes photos" / "Tu reviens avec la pêche et tout le monde en profite." When the activity benefits the family, the family becomes your ALLY, not your obstacle.
