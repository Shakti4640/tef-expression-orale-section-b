ğŸ”Ÿ KEY TAKEAWAYS FROM CHAPTER 2

1. Four criteria, equal weight: Lexical Range (25%), Grammatical Accuracy (25%), Interaction Quality (25%), Coherence & Fluency (25%).

2. Lexical Range rewards VARIETY (synonyms), PRECISION (right word for context), and IDIOMATICITY (expressions a French person would use). Aim for 6-10 idiomatic expressions across the conversation.

3. Grammatical Accuracy rewards COMPLEXITY as much as correctness. A candidate who attempts complex structures with minor errors scores HIGHER than one who uses only simple structures perfectly.

4. You must demonstrate 5-8 complex grammar structures: conditional, subjunctive, future simple, past tenses, relative clauses, comparatives, indirect speech, gerund.

5. Interaction Quality is about RESPONSIVENESS (answer what they actually said), TURN-TAKING (do not monologue), and DIALOGUE MANAGEMENT (ask questions, use backchannels).

6. End every turn with a question. Target 3-5 questions across the conversation.

7. Coherence requires LOGICAL ARGUMENTS (not random lists of facts) connected by MEANINGFUL TRANSITIONS.

8. Fluency is about SMOOTHNESS, not speed. Use fillers strategically. Never use the same filler more than three times.

9. Self-correction earns points. If you catch a grammar mistake, fix it out loud.

10. Know your scoring profile (Bookworm, Socializer, Robot, Natural) and focus your preparation on your weakest criterion.
